Shared Space

For my third year Design Studio, I was challenged with creating both a masterplan, along with a small group, and a full building design of an affordable housing scheme in Toronto’s Downtown East. This project involved secondary reserach, long term strategy, and iterative design. The buildings consists of 3 primary housing typologies designed for young families, vunerable youth, and single’s seeking co-living solutions.

Timeline: 4 Months

Results:

Our team revieved positive feedback for our unique demographic specific strategy in our master plan review. During the final building review, my project was commended for its care towards the existing neighbourhood and context, as well as the design of the shared public areas.

Background

The initial research for the project consisted of a wide evaluation of the curent state of the site. This included the existing services available to residents, existing designs, available greenspace, and current density. After evaluating the site, our team hypothesized demographics for 5 different availble locations given in our design brief. These included women, small families, elderly, and Co-ed Living.

In our physical analysis of the neighbourhood, we split the site into multiple layers of circulation patters, building density, public greenspace, and water systems. We found that the existing site struggled in providing a pedestrian experience from its lack of connectivity from streets to lanes, water management, lack of appropriate density for the population growth, and adequate public greenspace.

Project Context

Located on Sherbourne Street within Toronto’s Downtown East this project is designed for an area which has a range of services, demographics and architectural typologies. Specifically, this proposal is in response to the current urgent housing need for vunerable populations such as young families, at-risk youth, elderly, and homeless people. The overall masterplan encompasses 5 developments on Sherbourne street for multiple demographics, and each of these midrises uses principles of infill developments to prioritize community integration and social interaction. The masterplan also considers site connectivity from a sustainability perspective with water retension systems.

Phase 2: Building Design

Shared Space is a mixed-use building, on an existing parking lot, which is designed for new families, adults seeking co-living, and transitional youth housing. The project meets the community through porosity within the timber pedestrian pathways and both public and private shared spaces across multiple elevations that face a large community square.

Photo by Sven Kucinic on Unsplash

Based on my research surrounding youth homelssness, I created the persona of Aaron Mirotta. He is a 16 year old who is interested in the youth shelter program housed in Shared Space.

His primary goals are finding a safe community to support him as he tries to build a future for himself where he can be independent and take care of himself. He intends to finish high school and is interested in learning about trades. His pains include negative past experiences at shelters which led him to volatile situations on the street and also makes him aprehensive of entering another shelter situaton. He also feels isolated since leaving his old friends on the street in order to pursue his goal of finishing high school and lack of supportive family to reach out to for help.

His needs and desires include finding a place where he is able to both have his own space, and be social within other groups of youth. He wants to learn the necesary life skills for his future, and feel supported in his community. He also wants freedom in his spaces, as well as a sense of home that he currently lacks.
